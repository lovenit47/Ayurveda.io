WHAT IS AYURVEDA

Ayurveda is one of the world's oldest holistic healing systems. It originated over 5000 years ago, and is even more relevant today than ever before.

Unlike Western medicine, Ayurveda is a holistic science and looks at wellness as a whole: perfect health is defined as a state of balance between mind, body, spirit, and social well-being.

Rather than treating only the symptoms, Ayurveda focuses on finding and treating the root cause of disease.

Any type of disease (whether that is physical, mental, emotional, or spiritual) is caused by an imbalance (more about imbalances later). Thousands of years of Ayurvedic knowledge and wisdom have been documented in ancient scriptures, the most revered one being the Charaka Samhita.

Being a holistic science, Ayurveda can help you balance and improve several aspects of your life, including (but not limited to):

At its root, the Ayurvedic philosophy helps you to:

  • Naturally synchronise with your truest self
  • Nurture and nourish your mind, body and spirit equally
  • Improve your sense of well-being
  • Enhance health at a cellular level
  • Harmonize your energy with your environment

Ultimately, focuses on promoting a healthy and satisfying life experience that is free of negative emotions and aspects by supporting a natural balance.

WHAT IS MEANT BY 'BALANCE' IN AYURVEDA?

The concept of 'being in balance' is an integral and the core principle in Ayurveda which guides every other facet and even determines your relationship with this science. Perfect health, according to Ayurveda, is a balance between the body, mind, soul, and social wellbeing. Every thought and practice in this system, therefore, mirrors the dual principles of balance and connection.

In fact, Ayurveda is best characterized as the science of living in tune with nature's laws. Ayurvedic philosophy of natural and holistic medicine encourages people to live a balanced existence by making healthy and natural lifestyle adjustments.

Ayurveda's age-old knowledge is just as applicable or maybe even more relevant today as it was in the ancient times.

What are Doshas?

According to Ayurvedic philosophy, everyone is born with a unique constitution known as Prakruti.The Prakruti is a unique combination of physical and psychological traits that determine how each individual works. It is established from conception.


Each person's unique Prakruti, or constitution, is made up of three qualities known as Doshas. Ayurvedic ideology holds that the 5 natural elements of nature come together to form the 3 doshas, each of which has a distinct influence on body processes.


The unique constituent with which you're born is your natural state of body and mind. An individual's fundamental prakruti remains constant throughout their lives.

However, internal, external, and environmental variables like day and night, seasonal changes, nutrition, lifestyle choices, and more have an impact on one's prakruti that results in ailments, diseases and other visible symptoms.

The doshas are biological energies that may be found throughout the human body and mind. They regulate bodily and mental functions and give each living creature a unique blueprint for health, happiness and fulfilment.

Ayurveda, therefore, emphasizes sickness prevention and advocates sustaining health by adhering to daily and seasonal routines that help you regain the balanced constituent with which you were born.

Therefore, like all holistic health systems, Ayurveda focuses on the unshakable connections between the body, mind, and spirit. However, as you can see, Ayurveda's connectedness extends far beyond the individual, reaching into the universal.

AYURVEDIC BODY TYPES : THE THREE DOSHAS

Ayurvedic practitioners believe that dosha imbalance causes symptoms that are specific to that dosha and distinct from symptoms caused by other doshas. For example, if the aggressive and "hot" pitta-prominent individual aggravates pitta, he or she may acquire a prickly rash or have an acidic stomach.

A bad diet, too much or too little physical or mental exertion, lifestyle, pollution, aging, and other things can all contribute to an imbalance.

The whole cosmos, according to Ayurvedic philosophy, is an interaction of the five great elements' energies that form the 3 doshas:

Vata is represented by the elements of air and space. Vatta is in charge of controlling muscle and joint movement, as well as respiration and pulse. Vatta also regulates anxiety, fear, pain, and other nervous system processes.


Pitta is represented by fire and water, and it is considered to be in charge of digestion, metabolism, intellect, and skin color. Anger, hatred, and jealousy are all Pitta emotions.


Kapha is represented by the earth and water. Kapha governs the physical structure of the body as well as the immunological system. Calmness, forgiving, love, and greed are all believed to be Kapha-controlled emotional reactions.

THE 3 PILLARS OF AYURVEDA

Beyond the doshas and their manifestations, Ayurveda also prescribes 3 basic foundations on which you can base everything in your life. These initiate better and more effective functioning and even help adapt your lifestyle to your surroundings to live in a more grounded manner.
According to Ayurveda, to enjoy optimal health and a calm state of mind, the body must be supplied with:

  • 'Aharaa' or proper food
  • 'Nidraa' or proper sleep
  • 'Bhramacharya' or proper management of energy.
Ahaara - Pillar of food

Eating a good, wholesome meal allows your body to rightly absorb all nutrients and eliminate all toxins. Eating right is also important as it also contributes to 'prana' or the life force that sustains life.

In Ayurveda, this pillar also extends to:

  • Proper digestion and mindful consumption of food
  • Eating food at the right time and in the right combinations, and
  • Choosing food that suits your individual constituent type.

So, Ayurveda teaches us that paying attention to what we eat and how eat can have a significant effect on both our body and mind to attain the ultimate level of balance and harmony.

Nidraa - Pillar of Sleep

Sleep is body's natural way to heal, repair and restore energy. Ayurveda also throws light on how sleep is also the body's innate mechanism to cope and manage the chemicals and pollutants, and the stress and anxiety your mind undergoes while being awake.

As with food, just getting sleep is not enough. Having a sleep routine or sleep cycle that complements your daily life and your living environment based on your specific dosha type is what helps you lead a focused and active life.

Bhramacharya - Pillar of Energy

Ayurveda maintains that the circle of life isn't complete unless this particular pillar is covered too.

Although 'Bhramacharya' literally translates to celibacy, what Ayurvedic ideology implies here is that one should have a centred approach towards life by being aware of one's thoughts and actions.

Ayurveda believes that this is necessary to prevent us from overindulging or limiting ourselves from life’s most essential activities. It also helps us conserve our energies to completing tasks that add value to our lives. Thus, it’s nothing but mastering the technique of focusing where and to what you let your energy dive into.

AYURVEDIC SKINCARE

Ayurvedic skincare and natural beauty have been a part of Ayurvedic study and practice ever since it developed 5000 years ago. Much more than that, there is also evidence of advanced self-beautification notions and a wide range of cosmetics used by both men and women in antiquity in ancient India. The ancient Indians' conception of cosmetics and their application was based entirely on natural resources.
